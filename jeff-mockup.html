WHY MEDIATE A DISPUTE?

Mediation is a non-binding alternative dispute resolution process. The mediator has no power to make any decision or impose any result on those in the dispute. The mediator helps facilitate discussions about the issues in dispute and assist the parties in a mutually acceptable settlement. Mediation gives the parties control over the negotiation process and, most importantly, the result. Unlike a trials or arbitration, where the decision making is handed over to a judge, jury or arbitrator, the parties involved in the dispute work toward and craft a mutually acceptable settlement agreement. Mediation gives the parties a say in the settlement process. The parties and their legal representatives are given the opportunity to present their views of the case directly to the opposing parties and their legal representatives. It gives those involved in the dispute the opportunity to clear the air. Mediation can occur relatively early in the process of dispute. In busy judicial centers, trial can be several years away; mediation provides an early opportunity to discuss the issues in dispute and potentially resolve the dispute.  If settlement is achieved at mediation, the result is clearly known, understood and acceptable to the parties who have entered into the settlement agreement. Litigation and arbitration are uncertain. In each and every dispute, each side is convinced that they are right and they will win. 50% of those people will be wrong.  Mediation is less expense relative to the overall costs of litigation and arbitration. There is no question that legal representatives spend considerable time and effort developing their respective cases in preparation for mediation; this cannot and should not be avoided as mediation is a very important step in the dispute resolution process. However, most mediations can be conducted in the course of a half or one day in a relatively friendly environment. Most lawsuits and arbitrations can consume weeks. Moreover, the costs of experts at trial or at arbitration can be avoided.
